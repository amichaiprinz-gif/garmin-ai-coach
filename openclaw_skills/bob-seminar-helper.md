## Seminar 2 — Empirical IO Seminar (Economics)
**Paper:** Berry & Waldfogel (1999) — "Free Entry and Social Inefficiency in Radio Broadcasting"
**Deck:** C:\Users\amich\Downloads\מצגת_רדיו.pptx (16 slides)
**Field:** Industrial Organization, Media Economics

**Paper summary:**
- Radio markets: free entry leads to too many stations targeting large audiences (duplicated formats), too few serving smaller niches
- Key result: social optimum has fewer stations than free entry equilibrium (business-stealing externality dominates)
- Empirical strategy: cross-market variation in population size as instrument for number of stations
- Main finding: markets with more stations have fewer listeners per station (wasteful duplication), not more diverse programming

**Key concepts to master:**
- Free entry equilibrium vs. social planner's optimum
- Business-stealing externality (negative) vs. variety externality (positive)
- Hotelling model applied to radio format space
- OLS bias when # stations is endogenous → IV with market size
- Welfare calculation: deadweight loss from excess entry

**How to help:**
- Explain model intuitions in simple terms (Hebrew or English)
- Help structure presentation slides logically
- Suggest which empirical tables to highlight and how
- Draft speaker notes for specific slides
- Anticipate seminar questions and prepare answers
